--- canonical EN ---

You've built a lot. Identity files (your **kontekst**). An inpacket system with folders and permissions (your **meje**). A skill. A customer-feedback report. An investor deck. A sales workbook. An enriched lead list with a spend cap (your **limit**). And a working dashboard you can click.

Nine lessons. One folder that started empty. No more previews — you've now run every surface: Chat, Cowork, and Code. This last lesson isn't new mechanics. It's the decision: what do *you* point this at first?

---

The real work after this walkalong is not another lesson. It's writing three lines for one task in your own company — the same three lines you saw on every demo at the open day.

Pull out the printed worksheet from the open day — *"Zaveza: eno opravilo, tri vrstice."* Three rows: **kontekst, meje, limit.**

WAIT: Pick one task you repeat every week. Not the biggest. Not the most important. The one that annoys you — the one that eats more than 30 minutes each time.

USER: one task

---

WAIT: Now the three lines for it.
- **Kontekst** — who's involved, what does "good" look like, what does the agent need to know to do this like you would?
- **Meje** — what may the agent touch, what must it never touch, when must it stop and ask you?
- **Limit** — how much may it spend, how many records, how far may it go before it checks in?

USER: three lines

ACTION: Write `outputs/moja-zaveza.md` with the task name and the three lines (kontekst, meje, limit), plus the operating mode they'd pick (assisted / supervised / scheduled).

ACTION: Write `next-steps.md` at the top of the folder — three concrete things to try this week, based on the task and three lines they wrote. One per day, ish. Specific outputs, not "explore."
