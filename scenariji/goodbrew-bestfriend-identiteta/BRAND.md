# Good Brew & Your Best Friend Brand Voice

## Three Words That Describe Our Voice

- Warm
- Craft
- Grounded

## Three Words We Are Not

- Corporate
- Cute
- Health-hype

## Tone Rules

- Default register: direct, human, slightly playful.
- We can be warm without sounding childish.
- We explain safety plainly. No clever wording around ingredients.
- We avoid startup jargon, wellness claims, and exaggerated sustainability language.
- We address readers as "you" in English and "ti" in Slovenian when the context is informal.

## Vocabulary

- We say "beer for humans, treats for dogs."
- We say "pre-hop spent grain" when safety matters.
- We say "dog treats" or "priboljški", not "nutrition" or "pet food".
- We say "less brewery waste" or "better use of brewing byproducts", not "zero waste" unless proven.
- We never say: "healthy", "safe for every dog", "vet-approved cure", "beer for dogs", "hops are fine", "alcohol-free beer treat".

## Safety Language

Allowed:

- "Made from spent grain separated before hops are added."
- "No hops, no alcohol, no xylitol."
- "Recipe reviewed with a veterinarian."
- "Check ingredients and ask your vet if your dog has allergies or special dietary needs."

Not allowed without explicit human approval:

- Health or nutrition claims.
- Medical claims.
- Claims about all dogs, all allergies, or guaranteed safety.
- Any change to recipe, label, or ingredient wording.

## Visual Voice

- Colours: warm amber, cream, charcoal, muted green.
- Imagery: real brewery, real taproom, real people with dogs, real product bags.
- Avoid: cartoon mascots, glossy corporate stock photos, fake wellness packaging.

## Example

> The beer is yours. The treat is theirs.
>
> We save part of the grain before hops enter the brew, then bake it into simple dog treats. No hops, no alcohol, no xylitol. Just one good reason to bring your best friend to the taproom.
