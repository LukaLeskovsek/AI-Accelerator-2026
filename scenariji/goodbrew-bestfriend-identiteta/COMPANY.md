# Good Brew & Your Best Friend

## What We Do

**One-liner:** Good Brew & Your Best Friend is a Slovenian craft brewery that turns pre-hop brewer's spent grain into dog treats.

**Slightly longer:** Good Brew brews small-batch beer for humans. Before hops are added to a beer batch, part of the spent grain is separated, dried, and baked into dog treats under a vet-reviewed recipe. The treats contain no hops, no alcohol, and no xylitol.

The point is simple: beer for humans, treats for dogs, less brewery waste.

## Our Audience

- Primary: Slovenian craft beer drinkers who own dogs.
- Secondary: gift buyers, taproom visitors, dog-friendly cafes, pet boutiques, and small hotels.
- Not our audience: people looking for medical pet nutrition, beer for dogs, or cheap commodity treats.

## Our Product

- Core brewery products: small-batch beer, taproom sales, webshop bundles, and seasonal releases.
- Dog treat line: small bags of baked spent-grain treats made only from grain separated before hops.
- Sales channels: taproom, webshop, dog-friendly events, select partner shops.
- Current stage: fictional early-stage brand with a working taproom, small webshop, and first B2B partner tests.

## Our Goals

- Reduce avoidable brewery waste without making exaggerated sustainability claims.
- Build a repeatable webshop and taproom bundle.
- Sign 10 B2B partner locations in Slovenia.
- Keep product communication credible, safe, and easy to understand.

## Non-Goals

- We are not a pet health or nutrition company.
- We do not claim the treats cure, improve, or support medical conditions.
- We do not make beer for dogs.
- We do not use hops, alcohol, xylitol, or post-hop brewing waste in dog treats.
- We do not change recipes, labels, or safety wording without human review.

## Constraints

- Safety rule: only pre-hop spent grain can be used for dog treats.
- Treat recipe is vet-reviewed, but every public claim still needs approval.
- Batch traceability matters: product copy must not hide ingredients, allergens, or production dates.
- Regulatory context: standard food-safety, pet treat labelling, and EU GDPR rules apply.
- Budget and time are tight: small team, practical automations only.
